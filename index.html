Exploring Named Entity Recognition on Repertorium Germanicum I

How do NER models with different linguistic and historical training backgrounds process highly abbreviated Latin regesta?

Yiyang Huang ·

View repository

Abstract

This project explores the use of named entity recognition (NER) on the Latin regesta of Repertorium Germanicum I. These texts differ substantially from the modern, standardized language on which many NER systems are trained: they are highly compressed, contain frequent abbreviations, and include inflected or Latinized forms of personal and geographical names.

This project applies three pretrained NER models to the XML-encoded texts and compares their outputs. Each model represents a different training contexts: general Latin, medieval documentray Latin, and modern multilingual NER. The aim of this project is not to find the best model, but to investigate how different NER systmes respond to the liguistic and editorial challenges of the historical regesta.
